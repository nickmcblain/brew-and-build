Brew & Build — a community for tech founder dads in Bristol

  • A small, informal group of startup founders who also happen to be dads
  • Based in Bristol, UK
  • We meet roughly every two weeks — mornings, coffee, parks, and sometimes a pub

If you're building something — a company, a side project, an actual product — and you're also raising kids or a expecting dad, you already know the specific weirdness of that combination. The 6am sprint before the school run. The investor call you take from a soft play car park. The guilt that comes from both directions at once.

Brew & Build exists because it helps to know other people doing the same thing. Not for networking. Not for deal flow. Just for the relief of sitting across from someone who gets it.

How it works:

  • Regular meetups, a coffee shop, or a park, depending on the weather.
  • People who can make it, show up. People who can't, don't. No pressure.
  • We talk about what we're working on, what's hard, what's going well, and whatever else comes up.
  • Sometimes we do an evening social — a meal or a few pints. Partners welcome at those.
  • There's no agenda, no speaker series, no mandatory introductions. It's just a conversation.

Think of it less as an "event" and more as "that group of mates who happen to all run companies and have kids."

Who it's for:

  • You're a tech founder, co-founder, or solo builder — at any stage, from idea to revenue
  • You have kids (any age — newborns to teenagers, all count)
  • You're in or around Bristol
  • You want honest conversation, not a pitch practice session
  • You don't need another LinkedIn connection. You need someone to have a coffee with who understands why you're tired.
  • Helps if you have an interest in AI and love learning

We're not exclusive about it. If you're a freelancer, a contractor building something on the side, or someone who just left a job to start something — you're welcome. The common thread is: you're building and you're parenting, and you'd like to be around others doing the same.

What people say:

"I didn't realise how much I needed this until I went to the first one. I'd been heads-down on my startup for months and hadn't spoken to anyone outside my house who understood what I was doing. Just being in a room with other people going through the same thing was genuinely helpful."

"It's rare to find a group that's low-ego and actually honest. People here talk about the real stuff — the cashflow stress, the partner negotiations about time, the school WhatsApp group you forgot to check. It's not a highlight reel."

"I've made two genuinely good friends through Brew & Build. Not contacts. Friends. That's worth more than any accelerator."
